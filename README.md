                            <td>**1. Definition**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** What is the smallest unit that shows all properties of life? **Explanation:** The cell is the **smallest structural and functional unit** of all living organisms. [attachment_0](attachment)</td>
                            <td>Students answer based on previous knowledge. Students note the definition.</td>
                            <td>Cell Definition: Structural and Functional Unit.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**2. Discovery**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** The cell was first observed and named by **Robert Hooke** in 1665 when he looked at cork slices.</td>
                            <td>Students note the name of the scientist and the year.</td>
                            <td>Discoverer: Robert Hooke (1665).</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**3. Functional Unit**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** Cells perform essential processes like growth, reproduction, and respiration, proving they are the functional units of life.</td>
                            <td>Students understand that every cell is a miniature functional system.</td>
                            <td>Cell functions: Growth, Reproduction, Respiration.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**4. Cell Numbers**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** Name an organism made up of only one cell. **Explanation:** Organisms are either **unicellular** (single cell, e.g., Bacteria) or **multicellular** (many cells, e.g., Humans).</td>
                            <td>Students answer: Bacteria or Amoeba. Students differentiate the two types.</td>
                            <td>Types of Organisms: Unicellular vs. Multicellular.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**5. Postulate 1**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** The first rule of the Cell Theory is: **All living organisms are composed of cells.**</td>
                            <td>Students repeat the first postulate.</td>
                            <td>Cell Theory Rule 1: All living things are made of cells.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**6. Postulate 2**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** Where do new cells come from? **Explanation:** The second rule is: **All cells arise from pre-existing cells.** (Cells divide to form new ones).</td>
                            <td>Students answer: From old cells. Students grasp the concept of cell continuity.</td>
                            <td>Cell Theory Rule 2: New cells from pre-existing cells.</td>
                        </tr>

                        <tr>
                            <td>**1. Main Parts**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** The cell structure is organized into the boundary (membrane), the main fluid (cytoplasm), and the control centre (nucleus).</td>
                            <td>Students understand the division of cell structure.</td>
                            <td>Three Main Parts: Membrane, Cytoplasm, Nucleus.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**2. Membrane Location**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** Which part acts as the skin of the cell? **Explanation:** The **Plasma Membrane** is the outer flexible layer. It separates the cell from the outside environment.</td>
                            <td>Students answer: Plasma Membrane. Students locate the membrane on the chart.</td>
                            <td>Plasma Membrane: Outermost layer ($\text{Animal}$ $\text{Cell}$).</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**3. Membrane Function**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** The membrane is **selectively permeable**. It is a gatekeeper, allowing only specific things to enter and necessary things to leave the cell.</td>
                            <td>Students grasp the concept of the membrane controlling movement.</td>
                            <td>Function of Membrane: Selectively Permeable.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**4. Cytoplasm**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** What is the fluid found between the nucleus and the plasma membrane? **Explanation:** **Cytoplasm** is the jelly-like fluid where all organelles and chemical processes take place.</td>
                            <td>Students answer: Cytoplasm. Students describe the cytoplasm's nature.</td>
                            <td>Cytoplasm: Jelly-like fluid ($\text{Site}$ $\text{of}$ $\text{reactions}$).</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**5. Nucleus Location**</td>
                            <td>**Explanation:** The **Nucleus** is the large, central, spherical part. It is the control center for all functions. [attachment_0](attachment)</td>
                            <td>Students locate the nucleus in the center of the diagram.</td>
                            <td>Nucleus: Large, Central (Control Center).</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>**6. Nucleus Function**</td>
                            <td>**Q/A:** Why is the nucleus called the brain of the cell? **Explanation:** The nucleus **controls all cell activities** (growth, division, repair) and carries the **genetic information**.</td>
                            <td>Students answer: Because it controls everything. Students list the two main functions.</td>
                            <td>Nucleus Function: Controls all activities, carries $\text{DNA}$.</td>
                        </tr>
